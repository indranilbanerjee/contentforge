# Article Structure Template — ContentForge

## Content Type: Article
**Target Word Count:** 1500-2000 words
**Target Reading Level:** Flesch-Kincaid Grade 10-12
**Minimum Citations:** 8-12 sources
**SEO Focus:** High (primary + 3-5 secondary keywords)

---

## Standard Structure

### 1. Title (H1)
- **Format:** Clear, benefit-driven, includes primary keyword
- **Length:** 50-70 characters (for SEO + readability)
- **Style:** Title Case or Sentence case (per brand preference)
- **Examples:**
  - "How [Primary Keyword] Transforms [Outcome] in 2026"
  - "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Goal] with [Primary Keyword]"
  - "The Complete Guide to [Primary Keyword]"

### 2. Introduction (150-250 words)
**Purpose:** Hook reader, establish relevance, preview value

**Components:**
1. **Opening Hook** (1-2 sentences)
   - Statistic, question, provocative statement, or relatable scenario
   - Must grab attention immediately
   - Should include primary keyword naturally

2. **Problem/Context** (2-3 sentences)
   - Why this topic matters now
   - What pain point or opportunity it addresses
   - Establish relevance to target audience

3. **Value Proposition** (1-2 sentences)
   - What reader will learn/gain
   - Specific outcomes or insights promised

4. **Transition** (1 sentence)
   - Natural bridge to main content
   - Can be a question or forward-looking statement

**SEO Requirements:**
- Primary keyword in first 100 words ✓
- Secondary keyword (if natural) ✓
- Internal link to related content (optional)

---

### 3. Main Body (1000-1400 words)
**Structure:** 4-6 major sections, each with H2 heading

#### Section Pattern (Repeat 4-6 times)

**H2 Heading:**
- Clear, descriptive, benefit-oriented
- Include secondary keyword where natural
- Format: Sentence case or Title Case

**Content (150-300 words per section):**
1. **Topic Sentence** — Introduce the point
2. **Explanation** — Develop the idea with details
3. **Evidence/Example** — Data, case study, expert quote, or research finding (with citation)
4. **Application** — How reader can use this information
5. **Transition** — Connect to next section

**H3 Subheadings (Optional):**
- Use for complex sections with multiple sub-points
- Helps readability and SEO
- Provides additional keyword opportunities

**Content Elements to Include:**
- [ ] Statistics with citations (1-2 per section)
- [ ] Expert quotes or research findings
- [ ] Real-world examples or case studies
- [ ] Actionable insights or takeaways
- [ ] Visual content suggestions (charts, graphs, images)

**Formatting:**
- Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences max
- Bullet lists: For features, benefits, steps (use sparingly)
- Numbered lists: For sequential processes or ranked items
- Bold: Key terms or important points (use sparingly)
- Links: To cited sources and related internal content

---

### 4. Practical Applications / How-To Section (200-300 words)
**H2:** "How to [Apply This Knowledge]" or "[Number] Steps to Get Started"

**Purpose:** Make content actionable

**Format:**
1. **Step 1:** [Action] — Brief explanation (2-3 sentences)
2. **Step 2:** [Action] — Brief explanation
3. **Step 3:** [Action] — Brief explanation
...

**Alternative Format:** "Key Takeaways" with bulleted action items

---

### 5. Conclusion (150-200 words)

**Components:**
1. **Recap** (2-3 sentences)
   - Summarize main points
   - Reinforce core message
   - Include primary keyword naturally

2. **Future Outlook** (1-2 sentences)
   - Where this topic is heading
   - Why it remains relevant

3. **Call to Action** (1-2 sentences)
   - What reader should do next
   - Can be soft (explore more content) or hard (contact us, download resource)
   - Brand-appropriate based on article type

**SEO Requirements:**
- Primary keyword in conclusion ✓
- Internal link to related content or resource ✓

---

### 6. References / Citations Section

**Format:** Numbered or bulleted list

**Style:** APA, MLA, Chicago, IEEE (per brand preference)

**Example (APA):**
1. Author, A. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
2. Organization Name. (Year, Month Day). Report title. https://www.example.com

**Requirements:**
- All inline citations must have corresponding reference
- URLs must be live and accessible
- Include access date for web sources (optional based on style)

## SEO Optimization Checklist

**Title & Headings:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title (H1)
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least 2 H2 headings
- [ ] Secondary keywords distributed across H2/H3 headings
- [ ] Heading hierarchy correct (H1 → H2 → H3, no skipping)

**Keyword Density:**
- [ ] Primary keyword: 1.5-2.5% of total word count
- [ ] Secondary keywords: 0.5-1% each
- [ ] Keywords used naturally (no stuffing)

**Meta Elements:**
- [ ] Meta title: ≤60 characters, includes primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description: 150-155 characters, includes primary keyword + value proposition
- [ ] URL slug: lowercase-with-hyphens, includes primary keyword

**Content Structure:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in conclusion
- [ ] 2-5 internal links to related content
- [ ] 8-12 external citations to authoritative sources
- [ ] Alt text for images (if included)

**Schema Markup Recommendations:**
- Article schema (required)
- Organization schema (if brand-focused)
- FAQ schema (if Q&A section included)

## Quality Standards

**Readability:**
- Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: 10-12 (college level)
- Flesch Reading Ease: 50-60 (fairly difficult)
- Average sentence length: 15-20 words
- Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences (3-5 lines on screen)

**Citation Standards:**
- Minimum 8 citations, prefer 12+
- At least 5 unique sources
- No more than 30% from single source
- All sources verified live and authoritative
- Citation density: ~1 per 150-200 words

**Brand Compliance:**
- Voice and tone match brand profile
- Terminology adheres to brand guidelines
- No prohibited terms or claims
- Required disclaimers included

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**
- Start with dictionary definitions ("According to Merriam-Webster...")
- Use cliché openings ("In today's fast-paced world...")
- Stuff keywords unnaturally
- Create walls of text (paragraphs >5 sentences)
- Make claims without citations
- End weakly ("In conclusion..." or "To sum up...")
- Overuse transition words (moreover, furthermore, consequently)

✅ **Do:**
- Start with a compelling hook (stat, question, scenario)
- Use conversational, natural language
- Break up text with headings and lists
- Support claims with data and citations
- End with clear takeaway and CTA
- Vary sentence length and structure
- Read aloud to test flow

## Example Article Outline

**Title:** "How AI Content Generation Transforms Marketing Workflows in 2026"

**Introduction (200 words)**
- Hook: "73% of marketing agencies now use AI for content production—up from 12% in 2024."
- Problem: Manual content production can't scale to meet demand
- Value: This article reveals how AI-powered workflows increase output 5x while maintaining quality
- Transition: "Here's what changed—and how to implement it."

**H2: The Content Production Bottleneck (250 words)**
- Traditional 6-8 person workflow is unsustainable
- Data on production time, costs, quality variance
- Citation: McKinsey research on content operations

**H2: How AI Multi-Agent Pipelines Work (300 words)**
- Overview of specialized agents (research, writing, fact-checking, SEO)
- Quality gates and feedback loops
- Citation: Google research on multi-agent systems

**H2: Real-World Results: 3 Case Studies (350 words)**
- Agency A: 5x output increase, quality scores stable
- Agency B: 60% cost reduction, 2x faster time-to-publish
- Agency C: Zero hallucinations, 95% citation accuracy
- Citations: Case study sources

**H2: Implementation: 5 Steps to Get Started (300 words)**
- Step 1: Audit current workflow
- Step 2: Define brand voice and quality gates
- Step 3: Choose AI tools (criteria)
- Step 4: Pilot with 1 brand
- Step 5: Scale systematically

**H2: Challenges and Limitations (200 words)**
- AI can hallucinate (mitigation: multi-layer verification)
- Brand voice drift (mitigation: persistent profiles)
- Cost at scale (mitigation: hybrid human-AI)
- Citation: Industry reports on AI challenges

**Conclusion (150 words)**
- Recap: AI doesn't replace humans, it augments workflows
- Future: Expect even more sophisticated agents
- CTA: Download our AI Content Workflow Checklist

**References (12 sources)**
- Mix of academic, industry reports, case studies, news
